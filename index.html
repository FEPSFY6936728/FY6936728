                        Quantum computing is an emerging technology that leverages the principles of quantum mechanics—such as superposition and entanglement—to perform computations far beyond the capabilities of classical computers. Unlike traditional bits, quantum bits (qubits) can represent multiple states simultaneously, enabling faster and more efficient processing for complex problems. This breakthrough has the potential to revolutionize fields like cryptography, drug discovery, climate modeling, and artificial intelligence. By exponentially increasing computational power, quantum computing could accelerate innovation and transform industries across the globe (Arute et al., *Nature*, 2019; IBM Quantum, 2024).
