Quantum computing represents a transformative advancement in computational technology, offering the potential to solve problems far beyond the reach of classical computers. This article explores the usefulness of quantum computing across various fields, including cryptography, materials science, artificial intelligence, and optimization. It discusses how quantum algorithms, such as Shor’s and Grover’s, demonstrate significant speed advantages for specific tasks. Despite current challenges like qubit instability and high implementation costs, ongoing research continues to enhance quantum hardware and error correction methods. The article concludes that while practical, large-scale quantum computing remains in development, its potential to revolutionize technology and industry is substantial. Quantum computing is a cutting-edge field that harnesses the principles of quantum mechanics to process information in fundamentally new ways. Unlike classical computers that use bits (0 or 1), quantum computers use qubits, which can exist in multiple states simultaneously. This allows them to solve certain complex problems—like optimization and cryptography—much faster than traditional computers. (Arute et al., *Nature*, 2019; IBM Quantum, 2024).
